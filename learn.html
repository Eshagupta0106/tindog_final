A study based on a survey of over a 1000 dog reported that:

  • Dog owners are generally confident and have higher self esteem. This is directly linked to owning a dog. Dogs love unconditionally, greet their master joyfully and fulfil the need for acceptance, all of which contributes to a person's self esteem
  • Owning and maintaining a dog needs time, effort and money, which requires a person to be unselfish. Dog owners are generally unselfish.
  • Another study found that owning a dog seems to have a significant effect on a person's attitude towards interpersonal relationships.
  • On dating patterns of dog owners Vs non dog owners: it was found that dog owners spend more on a first date, tend to be more open to exploring a relationship and more open with their own feelings.
  • Dog owners also seem to be more open to the idea of a "soul mate" (ie. someone they want to spend their life with) as opposed to non dog owners.
  • Dog owners were found to be more in touch with their softer emotions, more enthusiastic and able to expressing their feelings/emotions.
